How to Use the PDF Page Extractor

  1. Click "Choose File" and select the PDF you want to extract pages from
  2. The tool will display the total number of pages in your PDF
  3. Enter the page numbers you want to extract (e.g., "1,3,5-7" for pages 1, 3, and 5 through 7)
  4. Click "Extract Pages" to create a new PDF with only your selected pages
  5. Download your new PDF file containing only the extracted pages

Frequently Asked Questions

What page range formats are supported?

You can use individual page numbers (e.g., "1,5,10"), ranges (e.g., "3-7"), or combine both (e.g., "1,3-5,8,10-12"). Pages can be in any order, and duplicates are automatically removed.

Is there a file size limit?

Since processing happens in your browser, the limit depends on your device's memory. Most modern devices can handle PDFs up to 50-100MB without issues. Very large files may take longer to process.

Will the extracted PDF maintain quality?

Yes! The extracted pages are copied from the original PDF without any compression or quality loss. Text remains searchable, images stay at original resolution, and all formatting is preserved.

Can I extract pages from password-protected PDFs?

This tool works only with unprotected PDFs. If your PDF requires a password to open or has copy restrictions, you'll need to remove those protections first using the PDF owner password.

All processing happens in your browser. No data is sent to any server.
