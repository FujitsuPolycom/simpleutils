Frequently Asked Questions

What is EXIF data?

EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) is metadata embedded in photos by cameras and smartphones. It includes camera settings, date/time, GPS coordinates, device model, and more. This data is invisible when viewing the image but stored in the file.

Why should I remove EXIF data?

EXIF data can reveal your exact location (from GPS coordinates), when photos were taken, and what device you used. Removing EXIF before sharing images online protects your privacy and prevents exposing sensitive information like home addresses.

Do all images have EXIF data?

No. Images from digital cameras and smartphones typically have EXIF data, but screenshots, graphics created in software, and images downloaded from social media often have no EXIF or stripped EXIF data.

Does removing EXIF affect image quality?

No! Removing EXIF only strips the metadata from the file. The actual image pixels and visual quality remain completely unchanged.
