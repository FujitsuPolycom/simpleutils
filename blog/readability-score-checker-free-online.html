Readability Scoring Methods

Flesch Reading Ease: 0-100 scale. Higher scores indicate easier reading. 60-70 targets general audiences. Below 30 is very difficult. Above 80 is very easy.

Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level: Indicates U.S. school grade needed to understand text. Grade 8 targets general public. Grade 12+ suits academic/professional audiences.

Gunning Fog Index: Estimates years of formal education needed. Lower numbers indicate clearer writing. 12-14 is acceptable for general audiences.

SMOG Index: Simple Measure of Gobbledygook. Estimates years of education needed based on polysyllabic words.

Coleman-Liau Index: Grade level calculation based on characters rather than syllables, useful for computer-based analysis.

Improving Readability

Shorter Sentences: Break complex sentences into two or three simple ones. Aim for 15-20 words per sentence average.

Simpler Words: Replace complex vocabulary with common alternatives. "Use" instead of "utilize." "Help" instead of "facilitate."

Active Voice: "We delivered results" is clearer than "Results were delivered by us."

Shorter Paragraphs: Web paragraphs should be 2-3 sentences. Long paragraphs intimidate readers.

Remove Jargon: Industry terminology alienates general readers. Explain necessary technical terms.

Target Audiences

General Public: Target grade 7-8 reading level. Most newspapers write at this level for maximum accessibility.

Professional Content: Grade 10-12 acceptable for business audiences comfortable with industry concepts.

Academic Writing: Higher grade levels acceptable, but clarity still matters. Grade 14-16 for research papers.

Legal/Medical: Technical precision sometimes requires complex language, but explanatory sections should remain accessible.
