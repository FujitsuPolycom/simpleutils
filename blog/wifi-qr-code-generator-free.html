How WiFi QR Codes Work

WiFi QR codes encode your network name (SSID), password, and encryption type in a standardized format that mobile devices understand. When someone scans the code with their phone's camera, the device automatically recognizes it as WiFi credentials and offers to connect. One tap completes the process - no manual entry required.

This works natively on modern iPhones and Android phones. No special apps needed - just the built-in camera app that already scans QR codes for websites and other purposes.

Security Considerations

WiFi QR codes are as secure as the password they encode - displaying them is equivalent to posting your WiFi password visibly. Use them for guest networks separate from your primary network. For businesses, ensure guest WiFi is isolated from company systems and data.

Consider where you display QR codes. Inside your home or business is fine. Visible from windows or public areas could allow unauthorized access. For temporary events, use time-limited guest networks and change passwords afterward.

Troubleshooting Connection Issues

If devices won't connect after scanning: verify the password is correct in the generator, ensure network encryption type matches router settings, confirm the SSID exactly matches including spaces and capitalization, and check that the guest device supports the WiFi standard (2.4GHz vs 5GHz).
