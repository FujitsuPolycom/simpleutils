What Makes a Password Strong?

Strong passwords have three characteristics: length (longer is exponentially harder to crack), randomness (unpredictable patterns), and character diversity (uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols). A 16-character truly random password takes centuries to crack with current technology.

Weak passwords include dictionary words, personal information (birthdays, names, addresses), common patterns (123456, qwerty), and keyboard walks (asdfgh). These fall within minutes to automated cracking tools.
