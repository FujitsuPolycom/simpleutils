Why Test Subject Lines?

Subject lines are the first and often only impression your email makes. Even valuable content goes unread if the subject fails to capture attention. Subject line testing identifies problems before sending: spam triggers that route emails to junk folders, length issues that truncate on mobile, weak emotional hooks that fail to engage, and clarity problems that confuse recipients.

Subject Line Best Practices

Be Specific: "Your order #12345 has shipped" outperforms "Order update" because it provides relevant detail.

Create Urgency: "Sale ends tonight" motivates action better than "Sale happening" - but avoid false urgency that erodes trust.

Front-Load Keywords: Put important information first since subjects truncate: "50% off laptops" not "Don't miss this opportunity for 50% off laptops."

Use Numbers: "5 tips for better sleep" often outperforms "Tips for better sleep" - numbers provide concrete expectations.

Ask Questions: "Ready to save money?" engages readers more than "Save money" statements.

Marketing Tip: A/B test subject lines on small audience segments before sending to entire lists. A 5% increase in open rate can dramatically impact campaign ROI for large lists.

Avoiding Spam Filters

Spam filters analyze subject lines for patterns associated with unwanted mail. Avoid ALL CAPS (seems like shouting), excessive punctuation (!!!), spam trigger words (free, guarantee, winner), deceptive practices (RE: on new emails), and misleading claims (You won! when they didn't enter contests).

The tool identifies specific spam triggers in your subject and suggests alternatives that maintain impact while improving deliverability.

Mobile Optimization

Over 50% of emails are opened on mobile devices. Mobile email clients display only 30-40 characters of subject lines. The tool shows exactly how your subject appears on popular mobile email apps, helping you front-load important information and avoid truncation at critical words.

Industry-Specific Advice

E-commerce: Include product names, sale percentages, or order status. "Your Nike shoes are on the way" beats "Shipping notification."

B2B: Professional, value-focused subjects work best. "How [Company] increased leads 40%" outperforms hypey alternatives.

Newsletters: Consistency helps - branded subjects or numbering (Weekly Update #47) build recognition.

Transactional: Clear, informative subjects improve user experience and deliverability: "Password reset for yoursite.com."
