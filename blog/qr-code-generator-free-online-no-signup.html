Why Use QR Codes?

QR codes eliminate typing. Instead of manually entering URLs, WiFi passwords, or contact details, users simply scan and access information instantly. This reduces errors, speeds up connections, and creates seamless experiences. QR codes work on business cards, product packaging, posters, restaurant menus, event tickets, and anywhere physical meets digital.

The pandemic accelerated QR code adoption for contactless menus, payment, and check-ins. Now they're universal - most phone cameras scan QR codes natively without additional apps, making them the easiest way to share digital information in physical contexts.

QR Code Types

URL/Website: Link to websites, landing pages, portfolios, or online resources with a scan.

WiFi: Share WiFi credentials without revealing passwords. Guests scan to connect automatically.

Contact Info (vCard): Share complete contact details. Scanning adds you to the user's contacts instantly.

Plain Text: Share messages, serial numbers, product codes, or any text information.

Email: Pre-filled email addresses, subjects, and messages. Scanning opens the user's email app ready to send.

SMS: Pre-composed text messages to specific numbers for feedback, contests, or automated systems.

Phone: Direct dial QR codes for support lines, business contacts, or emergency numbers.
